Physical

Guide-through of my Physical DofE section, Weekly Tennis.




I chose my Physical DofE section as my 6-month long section. I love playing tennis and that's one of the main reasons I chose to play tennis for my DofE section. I did and still do Tennis every Tuesday and Saturday at West End Lawn Tennis Club where I'm assessed by the Head Coach Bailey Harkness. Tennis is a great Cardio-Vascular sport, getting your heart pumping in no time. It's a sport of great techniques and coordination and requires a lot of effort to practise. Focusing on techniques, I chose my main goal for this seciton to be able to pickup Backhands more efficiently, and I'm still working to do so.







Tennis has helped me out a lot. It has helped me to gain confidence in myself and my skills. Tennis allows me to think positively of myself whenever I play, e.g. if I win a hard played match, or maybe even score a perfect winner. Not only have i developed mentally by playing tennis, I have also developed physical capabilities such as cardio vascular endurance(stamina) through the constant running and competition to get the ball at all ends of the court. It has also made me quite flexible as I may have to do some stretches to get maybe some volleys, thus even making me much more agile than before. Tennis has also helped me to learn to concentrate as well as boost my instincts, specifically in vollies. At the end of the day, I'm proud to be fit in all forms and what helped me to achieve this was Tennis through DOFE.

Volunteering

Guide-through of my Volunteering DofE section, Assistant Coaching.




I chose to help out my local Tennis club by helping the Coach teach children aged under 10. Although the growing number of children coming to play tennis biweekly at our local court, the amount of supervisors was in decline. So, I decided to volunteer to help the main Coach once every week on Saturday mornings. I helped the Coach to maintain the court after each 20 minute drill, fed balls to children so they could keep playing, ppicked up balls after every drill, and rallied with some of them to keep their positive charisma going on throughout their lesson, because without motivation, nothing can be achieved. I believe I did a great job.







My main goal for volunteering was to get rid of my social anxiety. I am quite a socially-awkwards person and starting a conversation is not my type of thing. I decided that I would gain confidence in myself and talk to people without any problem. If there'd be any issues with someone's e.g. form, I would point that out, if some did great, I would compliment them. Before I knew it, I found confidence in myself talking to other people and it was no longer awkward for me to convey what I felt. I loved helping out and motivating the trainees and benefitted myself on the way. I also explored respect for those elderly to you as I got to work with many Coaches, who all had different mindsets and beleifs, making me widely open to much more than what is my daily usual.
